Root Cause Analysis Report

Manager-level review of 108 aged cases (15+ days)  ·  IST Backlog  ·  May 2026

Executive Summary

📊
108 of 462 cases (23%) are aged beyond 15 days

41 are critical (>25 days, avg 41.6 days old, max 84 days). 67 are at-risk (15–25 days). The backlog has systemic gaps in escalation hygiene and customer engagement tracking.

🚨
60% of aged cases have no GUS Investigation logged

65 out of 108 aged cases have no engineering ticket — meaning no structured path to resolution. These are stalling purely due to lack of escalation.

📝
Reason for Delay was blank on 105/108 aged cases before this review

No documented accountability existed. Without a tracked reason, managers have no visibility into why cases age, making systematic intervention impossible.

Root Cause Breakdown 108 aged cases

🔬
Active Investigation — 35 cases (32%)

GUS Investigation logged, engineering engaged. Delay is due to complexity of the issue. These are correctly escalated but need periodic status checks to avoid stagnation.

🛠️
Troubleshooting in Progress — 34 cases (31%) Highest Risk

No GUS escalation despite 15+ day age. Engineers are still in early triage. 12 of these are >40 days old with no ticket — requires immediate manager review and escalation decision.

⏳
Pending from Customer — 32 cases (30%)

Cases where solution has been provided or more information has been requested. Aged because customer has not responded. These need a follow-up cadence and closure if no response within SLA.

⚙️
TMP (Engineering) Slack — 7 cases (6%)

Both GUS Bug and GUS Investigation filed. Actively tracked with Engineering. Delay is external — waiting for Engineering fix or patch. Lowest risk category; monitor fix ETAs.
