Executive Summary

📊
173 cases reviewed — all aged 8+ days, all reasons now documented

34 critical (>25d), 60 at-risk (15–25d), 79 early-risk (8–15d). Every case has a Reason for Delay filled. The data reveals five clear root cause patterns — each with a defined action.

The defining systemic failure. Troubleshooting (57/58), TMP-Slack (34/37), Pending Customer (28/36), and all 10 Support Delay cases lack engineering accountability. No ticket = no SLA, no owner, no resolution pipeline.

18 cases with no-call-attempt documented in engineer comments

Engineers explicitly wrote they made no customer contact: "No call attempt from April 22nd," "call attempt was very minimal," "customer was not followed from April 24th." These are self-reported gaps.

⏳
Only 20 of 77 cases escalated within 5 days — avg 12 days, 15 cases took 21+ days

Slow escalation directly drives case aging. 43% of cases took >10 days to reach Engineering. TMP-Slack cases spend ~12 days in informal Slack threads before any formal action is taken.
